Terms and Conditions

Please read these guidelines carefully before using the library services and resources.

1. Acceptance of Terms

By registering as a borrower or utilizing the physical and digital spaces of the Holy Trinity University Library, you agree to comply with and be bound by the rules, regulations, and legal frameworks outlined below.

2. Compliance with Philippine Laws

Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012)

In accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012, all personal information collected during registration (such as Student ID, name, email, and contact number) will be processed solely for managing book circulations, logs, and account updates. Your information will remain confidential within the university archives and will not be shared with outside third parties without explicit consent.

Republic Act No. 8293 (Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines)

Users must strictly adhere to Philippine copyright laws. Unauthorized duplication, photocopying of entire textbooks, distribution of digital scans, or modification of academic materials without proper license is prohibited. Offenders will face academic disciplinary actions.

3. Library Card & User Responsibility

  • Membership is strictly non-transferable. You may not lend your Student ID or borrower profile to another student to check out materials.
  • Users are completely responsible for any damage, loss, or defacing of books borrowed under their account name.
  • Any lost items must be reported immediately to the front desk to avoid compounding daily penalty fines.

4. Borrowing Policies & Delinquencies

Students are permitted to borrow a maximum of three (3) books at any single time. Books must be returned on or before the stamped due date. Late returns will accumulate fine charges calculated daily per book, excluding official school holidays and Sundays.

5. Physical Space & Conduct

To preserve a proper study environment, students must observe the following library protocols:

  • Maintain strict silence within assigned reading and research zones.
  • Food, sugary drinks, and heavy snacks are not permitted inside book preservation aisles.
  • Laptops and mobile devices should be set to silent or used with headphones.

6. Amendments to Policies

The library administration reserves the right to amend, update, or alter these terms at any period to better align with university policies and updated national legislation. Notice of adjustments will be publicized via our announcements board.
